Re Acknowledgement of the Written Warning regarding the Makerere Internship Program Report

Dear Mr. Nate,

Thank you for your feedback and for clearly explaining the concerns raised in the written warning regarding the Makerere Internship Program Report.

I sincerely apologize for missing the agreed deadline, and more importantly, for not informing you of the delay before the deadline passed.

After reviewing the warning and reflecting on what happened, I understand that the real issue was not only the late report. The greater concern was the breakdown in communication, and how it affected planning, coordination, and decision-making across the organization.

The report was not a standalone task. It fed into wider discussions, planning, operational preparation, and the Talent Funnel initiative. By not giving an update about the delay, I left management without the visibility needed to adjust, re-prioritize, or put a contingency plan in place. I now see that this created avoidable difficulties for the people and teams who depended on that information. For this, I take full responsibility.

What I Have Learned

This experience has taught me several important lessons, both as a professional and as a manager.

A deadline is never just a personal target. Every commitment is connected to others. When one deliverable is late, it can affect planning, preparation, and execution in areas that are not always visible.

Communication is part of execution, not separate from it. An early warning about a possible delay is often more valuable than staying silent while trying to solve the problem alone. Timely communication lets leadership make informed decisions and gives the organization time to adapt.

An organization works as a connected system. People, processes, projects, and timelines all depend on one another, so a delay in one place can create ripple effects across many teams. This reminded me how closely my work supports the wider goals of the company.

Trust is built through openness. Trust grows when commitments are met, and it grows just as much when challenges are shared early. Going forward, I will place greater emphasis on transparency and proactive communication.

My Action Plan Going Forward

To raise my standard of execution and accountability, I am putting the following changes into practice:

  1. Communicate risks early. I will report any risk of delay as soon as I identify it, rather than waiting until the deadline is near or has passed.
  2. Set internal deadlines. I will set my own deadlines ahead of the official ones, creating buffer time for review and unexpected issues.
  3. Track progress closely. I will hold more frequent status reviews on time-sensitive work, so risks are identified and addressed early.
  4. Escalate promptly. Whenever I face a serious blocker, dependency, or resource constraint, I will raise it quickly so support can be arranged before it grows.

These steps are intended to improve my own performance and to strengthen the reliability of the teams I support.

My Commitment as a Manager

As a manager, I have a duty to help build a culture of accountability, communication, ownership, and transparency. I will share the lessons from this experience with the Uganda team.

I will reinforce that deadlines are commitments, that risks must be communicated early, that issues should be escalated before they grow, and that each person's work connects to the larger goals of the company.

One message I want to emphasize is this: success is not measured only by finishing tasks. It is also measured by how well we communicate, collaborate, and support the wider organization. Strong execution requires both delivery and visibility. My goal is to use this experience constructively, so that it leads to lasting improvement for me and for the team.

Closing

I genuinely appreciate the feedback shared in this warning. While it points to a mistake on my part, it has also given me a valuable opportunity to reflect, learn, and grow.

I accept full responsibility for the missed deadline and the lack of communication around it. I understand the expectations of my role, and I am committed to turning these lessons into real improvements in my planning, communication, execution, and leadership.

Thank you for your patience, guidance, and continued support. I remain committed to contributing positively to AIBOS, supporting our shared objectives, and upholding the standards expected of me as a manager. Please accept my sincere apology.

Respectfully,

Ambrose Alanda
