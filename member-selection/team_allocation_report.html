2Assumptions and Requirements

These are kept deliberately separate: requirements are what you directed; assumptions are what the team has inferred and would like you to confirm.

2.1Requirements — as directed

  • Invite the selected Uganda members to Japan as soon as possible.
  • Prioritise members of high trust for long-term collaboration; trust is weighted above current skill level.
  • Each member brought to Japan is the direct responsibility of an inviting member.
  • Establish three sales teams on the director / students / developers model.
  • Generate stable income from government contracts to cover the company’s running costs.

2.2Assumptions — to be confirmed

  • An overall baseline of approximately 30 people.
  • 10–15 members in Japan; 9–12 developers retained in Uganda; about 3–4 developers per team.
  • No Uganda office for roughly June–October; a university space is a possibility, but unconfirmed.
  • No C/D-rank developers based in Japan.
  • Initial entry on a tourist visa, with a work visa to follow (which may require an academic transcript).
  • Tier naming standardised as A / B / C / D.

3Proposed Organization Structure

Three teams are proposed. Each team comprises:

  • One Japanese director (Inaba-san, Matsumoto-san, or Nishinaga-san);
  • Two students based at the Kyoto office; and
  • Three to four developers (C/D-rank) based in Uganda, working remotely.

Proposed structure: one founder, three director-led teams in Japan (each with two Kyoto-based students), and Uganda-based development pods executing remotely.

How the structure operates

  • The directors and students lead sales, focusing on government contracts; the Uganda developers handle execution.
  • This is intended to produce stable cash flow — an indicative profit target of roughly $30,000–$40,000 — to cover the company’s running costs.
  • The talented members in Japan are thereby freed to focus on internal and moonshot projects, stepping in only when a team is at risk of missing a deadline.
  • Initially, the Japan-based members help train the Uganda developers as the structure stabilises.
  • With no Uganda office assumed for June–October, the retained members work remotely during this period.

Decisions We Need From You

  • Structure. Do you confirm the three-team structure and composition — one Japanese director, two Kyoto students, and three to four Uganda developers per team?
  • Assumptions. Do you confirm the headcount and office assumptions in Part 2 (≈30 total; 10–15 in Japan; 9–12 in Uganda; no Uganda office June–October)?
  • Japan headcount. Nine are confirmed for Japan (four already here plus five selected) — the low end of your 10–15 target. How would you like to proceed: re-approach the resignees (notably Fred), relax the target, or hold to best-of-the-best over filling every seat?
  • Fred’s transfer. Should the promised transfer be sent regardless of his final decision? (We have proceeded with sending it.)
  • Remote & non-compete (Masembe). Under a project-based (1099) contractor model, may a departed member work with AIBOS remotely while employed elsewhere in the same field? He would continue at current pay if the non-compete clause is removed.
  • Sequencing. Should the government-contract income be stabilised before pursuing moonshot projects?
