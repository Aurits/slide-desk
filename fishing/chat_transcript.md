# 1. Fishing Rods

A fishing rod is the primary tool used to cast bait or lures and fight fish.

## Main Parts
- Rod blank (main body)
- Reel seat
- Guides (rings)
- Handle/grip
- Tip

## How to Use a Fishing Rod

### Step 1: Assemble the Rod
- Connect rod sections if it is a multi-piece rod.
- Ensure guides are aligned.

### Step 2: Attach the Reel
- Secure the reel in the reel seat.
- Tighten locking rings.

### Step 3: Thread the Line
- Pass the fishing line through each guide.
- Start from the largest guide to the tip.

### Step 4: Tie the Terminal Tackle
- Attach hook, lure, jig, or sinker.
- Use reliable knots such as:
  - Improved Clinch Knot
  - Palomar Knot
  - Uni Knot

### Step 5: Cast
- Hold the rod with both hands.
- Open the bail (spinning reel) or release spool (baitcaster).
- Swing forward and release.

### Step 6: Retrieve
- Turn the reel handle steadily.
- Vary speed depending on fish species.

### Species Commonly Targeted

|-----------|----------|
| Light Rod | Wrasse, damselfish, small reef fish |

## Advantages

- Better control

## How to Use a Baitcaster

### Step 1: Adjust Tension Knob
- Tighten until lure falls slowly.

### Step 2: Set Brakes
- Start with higher brake settings.
- Reduce as skill improves.

### Step 3: Press Thumb Bar
- Hold spool with thumb.

### Step 4: Cast
- Swing rod.
- Release thumb pressure gradually.

### Step 5: Control Spool
- Feather spool with thumb.
- Stop spool before lure hits water.

### Step 6: Retrieve
- Turn handle.
- Reel automatically engages.

## Common Beginner Mistake
"Bird's nest" or backlash:
- Happens when spool spins faster than line leaves.
- Prevent by using thumb control.
